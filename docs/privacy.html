PrivacyPlain statement

Privacy
Policy.

Quiet Cycle is designed to be private by default. All data stays on your device. Nothing is transmitted, stored remotely, or shared with third parties.

Last updated·28 May 2026

01

Scope

What data the app handles

The app only touches data that you create or capture inside it.

  • Period start dates you log.
  • Day notes (flow, mood, symptoms) if you choose to record them.
  • Settings (default cycle / period length).
02

Storage

How that data is stored

Everything lives in the app's sandboxed local storage on your device, using React Native's AsyncStorage. There is no account, no login, no cloud sync, no server.

  • Deleting items from inside the app removes them permanently from your device.
  • Uninstalling the app removes all stored data.
03

Negative space

What is NOT collected or transmitted

It's worth being explicit about the absences, since those are the parts privacy policies usually elide.

  • No analytics or telemetry of any kind.
  • No advertising identifiers.
  • No third-party SDKs that collect data.
  • No account, no login, no user profile.
  • No network requests for your data — nothing leaves your device.
04

Reproductive privacy

Why this matters

In the United States and elsewhere, reproductive health data has become a legal-exposure issue. Quiet Cycle is structured so that there is no record outside your phone to subpoena or breach. If you want to remove all data, uninstall the app.

You can also clear individual cycles by tapping a date inside a logged period and choosing Remove period.

05

Children

Children's privacy

Quiet Cycle is a general-purpose utility and is not directed at children under 13. It does not knowingly collect data that could identify a user of any age — because it does not collect or transmit personal data at all.

06

Versioning

Changes to this policy

If material changes are made, the last updated date at the top of this page will change. Continued use of the app after an update constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
