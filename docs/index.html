v1.0.0·iPhone · iOS 16+

01

Why this exists

A period tracker that doesn't sell you out

The biggest free period trackers run ads, share data with advertisers, and store your cycle on their servers. In jurisdictions where reproductive health data has been subpoenaed, that's not theoretical — it's documented. Quiet Cycle is a deliberate counterweight: all data stays on your device, no account is required, nothing is uploaded.

02

Overview

How it works

Tap a day on the calendar to log a period start. The app records it, then predicts your next cycle from your history (using a rolling average of past gaps). Each day you can also add a note — flow, mood, symptoms — which stays alongside that date in your local storage.

  • Filled circles are logged period days.
  • Dashed circles are predicted period days.
  • A small dot under a date means there's a note saved for it.
  • The status card at the top shows where you are in your cycle.
03

Predictions

How accurate is the prediction?

Predictions get better with more history. With only one logged cycle, the app uses your default cycle length (28 days unless you change it in Settings). With two or more cycles, it computes a rolling average of the gaps between starts and uses that. Cycles outside the 18–40 day range are ignored as outliers.

04

Settings

Can I change cycle / period length defaults?

Yes — tap Settings in the top right. Average cycle length and typical period length can be adjusted. Once you have history, the app prefers your actual averages over these defaults.
